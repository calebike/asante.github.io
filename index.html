GRBL is steadily increasing its dependence on computer-generated information, processing and telecommunications. This dependence will continue to grow with GRBL as the Bank makes Information Technology a major part of its strategy ensuring that every individual, department and division is completely automated. This dependency on computers and telecommunications for operational support poses the risk that a loss of these capabilities could seriously affect the overall performance of the Bank. As Management Information systems become more widely applied and more managers, supervisors and staff gain access to utilize the computer systems, computing security will become a major issue and cannot be separated from security in general. Risks in computing security arise as a result of errors and omissions, staff dishonesty, fire, water and other natural disasters. However, the major threats could come from within the organisation and require the need for stringent internal controls to be implemented.
